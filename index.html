I am Xiaojin Ma (go by Jin), a graduate student in the Department of Psychological & Brain Sciences at Washington University in St. Louis. Advised by Dr. Richard A. Abrams, I study visual attention, cognitive control, and the interaction between perception and action. My research questions what factors affect attentional deployment across visual scenes, and how the distractive effect of salient-but-irrelevant sensory inputs can be inhibited. I work primarily in R and Python, and approach research questions with behavioral methods, eye tracking, and computational modeling.

I was originally from Shenzhen, China, and earned my B.S. in Psychology from South China Normal University in 2018.
